About the Developer

Nicholas Edwards is a Lieutenant Colonel in the California Air National Guard and an emergency management professional currently completing a Master of Science in Emergency Management at the University of Southern California. He is a Certified Business Continuity Professional (CBCP) through DRI International and a member of the National Emergency Management Association (NEMA).

His career spans nearly 25 years of active and reserve military service across intelligence, ISR operations, WMD consequence management, and all-hazards disaster response. He has served as a Remote Sensing Coordinator at Joint Command Posts during major wildfire incidents, commanded a federally certified WMD Civil Support Team responsible for CBRN reconnaissance and consequence management across California, and led national-level operations teams delivering real-time situational awareness to senior DoD and interagency leadership across all 54 states and territories.

Edwards served as National Program Manager of FireGuard, a remote sensing and geospatial intelligence capability built in response to the 2018 Camp Fire and scaled to support CAL FIRE, FEMA, and state Emergency Operations Centers during major disasters. He has trained emergency managers at the national, state, and local level and taught incident awareness and assessment courses across the country.

About NEXUS EOC

NEXUS EOC is a web-based emergency management training simulator that generates a unique, geographically accurate scenario for every session based on the selected incident type and jurisdiction. An AI engine evaluates player decisions against NIMS, ICS, ESF, and FEMA doctrine in real time, then delivers a comprehensive after-action review calibrated to the player's role and jurisdiction when they type ENDEX.

The platform supports 23 roles — including EOC Director, all four Section Chiefs, PIO, Safety Officer, and all 15 Emergency Support Function Liaison Officers — and six jurisdiction types from Rural County to Tribal Nation. Every session is different. No facilitator required.
